Website optimization and work queue

The purpose of the work queue is to strengthen the example site foundation, reduce the sense of hurry, make information easier to find, and ensure that content, reporting and service-page improvements move forward in a controlled way.

Next step: start with foundation cleanup. Centralize the instructions, document responsibilities, agree on a request channel and build the first service-page classification model.

Workstream groups

Six main workstreams

The groups make it easier to see why individual work queue items belong to the same core problem: the foundation of the work, decision-making and moving into action all need to be manageable.

1. Foundation cleanup and setup

Many other improvement tasks slow down if the basic information, instructions, responsibilities and ways of working are not in place.

2. Service page and site structure improvements

The roles, classification and review model of the service pages need to be clarified before larger fixes.

3. Making decision work and queues easier

Review work needs a view where the problem, rationale and next decision are in the same place.

4. Reporting and moving from data to action

Reports should help with prioritization, not just describe the data.

5. Content work, campaigns and timely highlights

Timely content needs a clear delivery model so urgency does not derail other improvement work.

6. Local visibility, locations and internal linking

Local discoverability and internal linking should be tied to service-page improvement.

Sprint selection rule

Sprint selection rule

Only a limited amount of work should go into one sprint. This protects focus and prevents foundation cleanup from getting buried under urgent work.

Sprint componentRecommended amountExample
Main goal1Foundation cleanup or the service-page classification model
Medium-sized support tasks2Responsibility matrix, search in the review view
Small support tasks1-2Instruction cleanup list, summer highlights checklist
Ongoing maintenanceAlways a small part of the sprintUpdating instructions, processes and responsibilities
Not included-Mass updates, automated CMS changes, overly broad campaigns
